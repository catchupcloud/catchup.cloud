CatchUp is now ISO 27001:2022 certified

Information security has become increasingly important in an increasingly digital society. Organizations worldwide are navigating the hash landscape of data breaches, cyber threats, and regulatory responsibilities to safeguard sensitive information.

ISO 27001 has emerged as a beacon of trust and reliability in this growing digital economy. The most recent version of the international standard for information security management is ISO 27001:2022, an upgrade from ISO 27001:2013. It gives organizations a structure for managing and protecting their information assets. The foundation of this standard is the risk management principle, which requires organizations to identify, evaluate, and address information security risks.

  • - why our organization chose to adopt it
  • - what it means for our customers


What is ISO 27001:2022?

ISO 27001:2022, the latest iteration of the globally recognized ISO 27001 standard for information security management systems (ISMS), provides a systematic approach to managing sensitive information, ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data. ISO 27001:2022 offers a comprehensive framework for organizations to establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve their ISMS.

The new version of ISO 27001 (upgraded from 2013 to 2022) brings crucial updates to the standard, aligning it with the rapidly changing landscape of information security. These updates include a broader perspective on risk management, emphasizing the importance of integrating security measures into an organization's processes and addressing new challenges brought by cloud computing and mobile technologies.
